## 3. Root-cause findings

### 3a. DIV was missing the momentum-reset gate — found and fixed live today

7 of today's 18 losses came from `DIV` re-firing repeatedly on the same symbols during a tight chop stretch (QQQ and IWM both moving in under a 0.25% band, 18:39-18:51pm ET): 4 separate QQQ entries, 3 separate IWM entries, each stopped by 0DTE leverage before the next one fired. Root cause: the same-symbol momentum-reset gate that already protects `IV` and `ORB` from exactly this repetitive-re-fire pattern was **never extended to `DIV`** when it shipped as a new signal type. **Fixed and deployed live today** (commit `5c6798f`) — confirmed no further entries fired in the 40+ minutes after the fix, through market close.

### 3b. A rare stop-market slippage event, confirmed as an outlier, not a pattern

One IWM 294C trade (10:01am ET) triggered its stop correctly at the intended $0.89 (15% threshold) but filled at $0.70 (33.3% adverse) due to a sub-second liquidity flash-dip in the underlying (IWM ticked $294.62→$294.21 in ~0.3 seconds, then recovered). Checked against all 21 hard stops since 7/16: this was the only one to land more than ~2 points past its intended threshold (20 of 21 landed within 11.4%-16.8%). Real risk, confirmed rare — a stop-limit order would cap this specific tail risk at the cost of introducing a new failure mode (order not filling at all on a real gap). Not yet built; candidate for next week.

### 3c. Tested three hypotheses today, all rejected by backtest evidence

- **ORB stop widened to 25%/30%** (theory: give momentum trades room to survive normal pullbacks) — both worse than the current 15%, 30% dramatically so (-$3,649 on ORB alone at 30%, vs -$58 at 15%, same 90-day window). **Not shipped.**
- **ORB intraday-VWAP trend gate** (theory: let ORB trade with the day's realized trend, not just the multi-month daily trend) — swung the 90-day backtest from +$3,139 to -$1,771. **Not shipped**, though the underlying finding that motivated it (see 3d) is real.
- **Capital-based daily cap** (theory: a $-budget cap is fairer than a flat trade count, since cheap losers shouldn't cost the same "slot" as expensive ones) — worse than count-based caps at every budget level tested ($800 and $2,000), because capital recycling on a fast-churn day let *more* total trades through, not fewer. **Not shipped.**

### 3d. Tested two market-prediction hypotheses directly, both come back negative for this dataset

- **"Buy the dip" on a gap-down-within-still-bullish-trend setup** (today's own morning setup) — tested across SPY/QQQ/IWM + Mag7, 90 days: n=66, avg return **-0.176%**, win rate 51.5% (no real edge, essentially a coin flip).
- **Daily RSI(14) oversold** — n=1 in 90 days (market's been too persistently bullish to test this meaningfully). **Daily overbought, shorted**: n=120, avg return **+0.075%** same-day and **+0.606%** over the next week — shorting overbought was a losing bet on both timeframes.
- The one thing that *did* show real signal: gap-**aligned**-with-trend continuation (gap up within an already-bullish trend), n=79, avg **+0.196%**, ~60% continuation rate — empirical support for trading with the trend, consistent with ORB's existing design philosophy.

## 6. Post-report findings (2026-07-17 evening through 2026-07-18)

Everything below happened after Section 1-5 were written, in the same continuous session, prompted by the user asking for a direct "signal-mix rebalancing" conversation given how flat every fix in Section 3c/3d looked.

### 6a. The equity-band bug — the single most important finding of the week

`computeContractCount` (`server/execution/optionPositionSizing.ts`) rejects new entries when account equity falls outside `[min_account_equity, max_account_equity]` — but nothing moves equity back inside that band once crossed (equity only changes via realized fills, and fills can't happen while blocked). This is a **permanent lockout, not a throttle**. Confirmed in a fresh 90-day backtest: the account crossed the then-live $5,000 ceiling on 2026-05-11 and went completely silent for the remaining ~2 months of the window. **Every positive backtest number reported earlier this week (including the scalper-ladder "+$3,139" result cited in prior reports) was actually "made enough to hit the ceiling by some date, then sat idle," not a true 90-day read.**

Fixed 2026-07-17: `max_account_equity` raised to effectively unlimited (100,000,000), `min_account_equity` dropped to a nominal $1 sanity floor. The TRUE, uncensored 90-day result for the full live signal mix (all 6 types, scalper ladder, 15% stop): **-$1,982** — not a win.

### 6b. Signal-mix rebalance — ORB, IV, STTF disabled

With the corrected equity bounds, nine different variants were tested to try to fix that -$1,982: raising ORB's confidence floor (0.60→0.70→0.75), a quiet-open window blocking IV/ORB before 10:30/11:00/11:30am, a confidence discount at :00/:30 marks (both blanket and ORB-scoped), a hard :00/:30 entry filter, and halving ORB/IV's position size. **All nine landed within a ~$70 band of the same ~-$2,000 loss.** The shared-capital-pool architecture reabsorbs whatever gets freed up by any single-signal fix — even halving ORB/IV's position size didn't help, because the freed buying power just let more of them fire.

**Disabling ORB, IV, and STTF entirely** (not gating tighter — removing them from the entry-loop rotation, `DISABLED_SIGNAL_TYPES` in `execute-alerts.ts`) turned the same 90-day window into **+$1,367**. DIV alone accounts for nearly all of it (+$1,439 standalone); DTTF/TTTF net close to breakeven; ORB/IV/STTF were the consistent drag at true scale despite occasionally looking like the best performer in earlier, equity-band-corrupted samples.

**Shipped live 2026-07-18**: only DIV, TTTF, and DTTF actively trade now. ORB/IV/STTF's logic remains in the code, untouched, just gated off.

### 6c. Order-placement errors were undiagnosable by design — now fixed

Every order-placement failure was logged via `String(e)` on an Axios error, which only ever produces the generic `"Request failed with status code 422"` — discarding Alpaca's actual response body. This is why the 403/422 failures flagged in the very first daily report (7/15) went three full days without a root cause: nothing ever captured what Alpaca actually said. Added `describeAlpacaError()` (`server/execution/alpacaClient.ts`), now used everywhere an order-placement error gets logged. The underlying 403/422s are still not root-caused (couldn't reproduce with markets closed), but the next occurrence will be immediately diagnosable instead of a dead end.

### 6d. Paper account reset

The user deleted the old Alpaca paper account 2026-07-17 evening (both trading and data API calls started 401ing simultaneously — the tell for "account deleted," not an auth bug). New paper account created same day: fresh $2,000 equity, $4,000 buying power, options level 3. New credentials swapped into `.env.local` and Vercel production env vars, followed by a manual redeploy to force-pick-up the change. Daily entry cap reverted from the weekend's uncapped (999) test back to 10.

### 6e. `pdl_bounce` fix re-validated on corrected infrastructure

The `pdl_bounce`/`pdh_rejection` touch-and-reclaim fix (shipped 7/16) was originally validated on the OLD, equity-band-bugged backtest, so that comparison was untrustworthy. Re-ran before/after on the corrected infrastructure: IV's standalone performance went from **-$1,542 (old proximity-only logic) to -$16 (fixed logic)**, a genuine +$1,526 swing with win rate improving from 41.8% to 53.7% — confirms the fix was real and effective. But the account TOTAL barely moved (-$1,998 → -$1,982) for the same shared-capital-pool reason as 6b. Since IV is disabled live right now, this is validation for the record, not something currently in effect.

### 6f. A Vercel deployment silently failed for ~24 hours

While shipping 6b live, discovered that `tsc` type-checks the whole repo including `scripts/` (dev-only backtest tooling, never actually deployed) — a file intentionally left uncommitted from Friday broke the build for every push after it, but `npm run build` passed locally the whole time (the uncommitted file was still present on disk). Vercel kept serving the last successful deployment with no obvious failure symptom, so **every code change from Friday afternoon through Saturday evening — including the DIV gate fix, the error-capture fix, and the initial equity-band fix commits — never actually reached production** until this was caught and fixed. The account being fine over that stretch was luck (last successful deploy already had the DIV fix baked in), not confirmation the later fixes were live. **Lesson for future sessions**: after any push meant to ship a live fix, check actual deployment status (`vercel ls`/`vercel inspect`), don't infer success from `git push` alone.
