## 3. Root-cause findings

### 3a. `pdl_bounce`/`pdh_rejection` was firing on proximity, not on an actual bounce — found and fixed today

`detectIVSignal`'s `pdl_bounce` fired whenever price sat within **1% of the prior-day low**, with no requirement that a bounce was actually happening. On a $750 stock that's a **$7.50-wide band** — price could be drifting steadily downward, never once touching the real level, and still fire a "bounce off support" signal at 85-98% confidence the whole way down. Checked against trade history: **22 of the last 24 `pdl_bounce` trades stopped out** across 2026-07-15 and 2026-07-16 combined, at confidence readings from 0.78 to 0.98 — confidence tracked **zero correlation** with outcome, which is what you'd expect from a model with no real signal to be confident about.

**Fixed today** (deployed ~1:41pm ET, after all 8 of today's `pdl_bounce` trades had already happened): the signal now requires the level to have actually been tested in the last ~30 minutes (a candle low/high pierced or came within 0.15% of it) **and** price to have already reclaimed the favorable side by more than noise. Verified live within an hour of shipping: SPY tagged $750.47 against a $750.20 PDL around 2:15pm ET and reclaimed to ~$751.3 — the system correctly held off on `pdl_bounce` on SPY specifically (its own MACD curl wasn't cooperating at that instant) but correctly fired it on the QQQ/IWM leg of the same move at 2:24pm (confidence 0.71, below the 0.80 gate, so no trade — appropriately conservative on one data point).

**Caveat**: zero real trades have tested the fix yet. Nothing cleared the entry gates for the rest of the session after it shipped (remaining signal activity was `or_rejection` at 0.45-0.68, well under the 0.80 floor).

### 3b. Why ORB never played the afternoon breakdown — a real structural finding, not a bug

Ran a full diagnostic replaying today's actual price/indicator data through the live ORB detection code. Result: **a bearish ORB continuation candidate (breakout below the opening range + bearish MACD curl agreement on SPY and/or QQQ) existed continuously from 12:05pm ET onward — almost the entire stepped decline — and was blocked by the daily-trend-alignment gate every single time.**

The reason: `isDailyTrendAligned` compares the trade direction against the **daily** EMA50/EMA200, not anything intraday. SPY's daily EMA50 ($737.26) sits comfortably above its EMA200 ($694.48) — a firm multi-month uptrend — so ORB's bearish continuation thesis is structurally locked out regardless of how clean today's intraday breakdown looked. Meanwhile the morning's bullish ORB candidates (9:35-11:50am ET) fired and were correctly aligned, matching the live alerts actually seen.

This is a real design tradeoff, not an oversight: the gate exists specifically so ORB doesn't chase counter-trend breakouts that are more likely to snap back (the "especially on supertrend days" thesis it was built around). Today it cost a legitimate, well-structured continuation trade. Worth considering: a **separate, shorter-window intraday trend check** (e.g. a 20/50 EMA cross on the 5-min chart) as an alternative or supplement to the daily-trend gate — letting ORB trade with today's *realized* move even when it runs against the longer trend. This needs to be backtested before touching anything live; it's a meaningful behavior change, not a tuning tweak.

## 4. Changes shipped today

1. **`pdl_bounce`/`pdh_rejection` fix** (detailed above) — touch-and-reclaim confirmation instead of bare proximity.
2. **New tier ladder live**: replaced the 10/20/30%(+100% runner) plan with a tighter 10/15/20/25/30% ladder, no runner at any contract count. A 90-day walk-forward backtest on the fixed signal (same window, same 15% stop, same entry gates — tier spacing was the only variable) swung the result from **-$1,508 to +$3,139**. Root cause: SPY/QQQ calls were repeatedly running up past the old wider tiers and reversing into the hard stop before the runner's open-ended target — or even the earlier fixed tiers — ever paid out. This is a 0DTE book; banking profit sooner and more often beat holding for a bigger, rarer move in every backtest variant tested today.
3. **Broker-side resting limit orders for tier/runner profit-taking** (built earlier in the session, ahead of today's live-fire): each tier now gets its own real resting sell order at entry instead of a bot-polled threshold check, closing the gap where a fast move could run through several tiers between polls.
4. **Daily entry cap**: raised from 5 to 10 as the new standing default; set to effectively uncapped (999) for the remainder of today specifically, as a live stress test. No new trades happened before the close either way — see caveat in 3a.

Confidence floors were **not** changed today: ORB 0.60, TTTF/DTTF/STTF 0.65, IV 0.80, unchanged in both live code and every backtest run.
