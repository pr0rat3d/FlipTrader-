## 3. Scrutiny: where this needs to get better

### Profit-taking & exit logic

- **Over half of all real trades (53.6%) ended in a stop, and the day was only profitable because three winners were large relative to many small losers** (+$444, +$183, +$94 vs. a long tail of -$10 to -$67 stops). This is a legitimate scale-out/runner profile, but it's fragile by construction: it depends on catching at least one or two real winners every session. **Recommendation**: start tracking "days where zero legs reach the first tier" as a leading indicator — that's what a genuinely bad day looks like under this structure, and it hasn't been observed yet.
- **IWM 296C and QQQ 716C were each bought and stopped out 5-6 separate times today**, all during the 2:40-2:55pm reversal/whipsaw window — individually small losses, -$67 and -$59 in aggregate. The same-symbol momentum-reset gate (added today) was supposed to prevent exactly this for IV, but **it didn't help here, and the reason is a real logic gap**: the check only requires the MACD histogram to have touched the *opposite* sign at some point since the last close — it doesn't require the histogram to then cross back into agreement with the *new* trade's direction. During this chop, IWM's histogram was persistently negative the whole time, which trivially satisfied a "reset" check for every new bullish re-entry without the market ever actually confirming a fresh bullish move. **Recommendation**: tighten the reset check to require a genuine zero-line recross in the new trade's own direction, not just a touch of the opposite sign at any point.
- Worth checking empirically: **how many of today's 28 fills hit tier 1 within 1-2 minutes of entry?** 0DTE gamma means a 10-15% premium move can happen almost immediately on a good entry — if tier 1 is firing near-instantly on a lot of trades, it's converting potential runners into small, premature partial exits more often than the tier ladder was designed for.

### Strategy & signal selection

- **100% of today's real capital went through one signal type (IV).** ORB — the strategy explicitly built for trend days — never got a single real trade, and its own base-confidence formula (single-index breakouts cap at 0.55-0.61) is why, independent of any downstream gate. Today's market gave ORB a legitimate test (a real midday move) and it still couldn't clear its own floor. **Recommendation**: revisit `orbBaseConfidence()` — either it's too conservative for single-index breakouts, or `min_confidence` should be type-specific instead of one global floor across all five signal types.
- **TTTF/DTTF/STTF is structurally unable to catch a reversal where the MACD confirmation lags the RSI-divergence peak** — confirmed live today (SPY's divergence peaked 2:36pm, MACD didn't cross until 2:48pm, by which point price was no longer at a new high so the divergence condition had already gone false again). A fix was scoped and approved today (widen `detectRSIDivergence`'s lookback, add a histogram-deceleration tier) but not yet built. This is the single highest-leverage pending item — it's the reason the entire realized book depends on one signal family.
- The 5-minute staleness cutoff (shipped today) fixes the worst case, but it's a **time-based fix for what's really a price-relevance problem** — a leg can be under 5 minutes old and still have price move meaningfully away from where the signal actually fired, especially in the kind of whipsaw seen this afternoon. Worth considering a price-distance check (current price within X% of the leg's `entry_price`) as a complementary gate, not a replacement.

### Risk management

- **No daily loss limit exists.** `execution_settings` has a per-position hard stop and a daily *entry* cap, but nothing that pauses new entries after the account is down some threshold on the day. Today closed positive, so this was never tested — but it's a real gap before real money, and worth building before the first day that opens with a string of stop-outs.
- **Order-placement reliability is still an open question**: 8×`403` and 3×`422` today, two different error codes, neither root-caused. The `403` pattern already caused $602 of real damage once this session (the OPRA-bars gap, since fixed) — an order-placement failure on a *stop* order, not just an entry, is the actual danger here, and it's already happened once.
- The hard stop and breakeven ratchet are **poll-interval, not tick-by-tick** (confirmed: monitor-executions.ts checks about once a minute, no internal sub-loop). Today's threshold tightening (30%→25% stop, breakeven→entry+5%) compensates for the ~5% of drift observed live, and was validated same-day on three real stop-outs. Worth re-checking after a few more sessions whether 5% of buffer is still enough, especially on a faster-moving day than today.

## 4. Fixes shipped today (for the record)

1. MACD-lookback rolling window + a math bug that silently clamped it (both from day 1, confirmed still live).
2. IV's target-price formula (was on the wrong side of entry for the majority of legs) — shared fix with ORB.
3. Hard stop switched from a never-working closed-candle bars check (OPRA agreement not signed) to a live-quote check.
4. Contract sizing switched from equity-based to buying-power-based budget.
5. Same-symbol+direction pyramiding guard, plus an opposing-signal close-all safety net.
6. Momentum-reset gate for IV/ORB re-entries (with a documented gap — see Strategy section above), TTTF/DTTF/STTF exempt, ORB has an 85%-confidence bypass for supertrend days.
7. Terminology: TTF/DTF/STF → TTTF/DTTF/STTF, with a historical-data backfill.
8. 2-contract tier plan: full exit by +30% (was an open-ended runner).
9. Hard stop 30%→25%, breakeven ratchet 0%→entry+5%.
10. 5-minute staleness cutoff on signal execution.
